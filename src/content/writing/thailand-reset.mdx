I'm lucky enough to spend time in Thailand each year, and I've started treating it as a reset rather than just a holiday.

This isn't about working harder. It's about creating space — to think more deeply about Editorial Intelligence, spend time with family, train, read and slow down.

Ironically, slowing down usually helps me see the bigger picture more clearly than another week of meetings ever could. Much of the thinking behind Editorial Intelligence didn't emerge at a desk. It came from noticing patterns when there was finally enough space to see them.

Long term, I'd like to split my year between the UK and Thailand: six months collaborating with clients, speaking and building in the UK, and six months writing, developing ideas and spending time with family in Thailand.

I don't see that as stepping away from work. I see it as protecting the conditions that produce my best work.

Sometimes the most productive thing you can do isn't squeeze another task into your calendar. It's create enough space for better ideas to appear.
