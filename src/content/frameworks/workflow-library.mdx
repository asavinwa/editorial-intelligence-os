Most editorial work is reinvented from scratch every time.

A brief arrives. Someone works out how to approach it. The work gets done. The approach is not recorded. The next time a similar brief arrives, someone starts from the beginning again.

This is how most content teams operate. Each project is a one-off. Process knowledge lives in people's heads. When people leave, the knowledge leaves with them.

The Workflow Library changes that. It captures how good editorial work is done — the processes, decision frameworks and quality standards that produce the best outputs — so that each iteration of the work starts from a stronger position than the last.

---

## What a workflow is

A workflow is a documented approach to a recurring editorial task.

Not a rigid procedure. An approach — a way of thinking through the task that has proven reliable, with enough structure to be repeatable and enough flexibility to adapt to context.

Good workflows capture three things:

**The inputs** — what signals, briefs, evidence or context the workflow needs to start from.

**The process** — the steps, decisions and judgement calls that turn inputs into outputs. Including where editorial judgement matters most, and what good looks like at each stage.

**The outputs** — what the workflow produces, in what form, and how it connects to the next stage of the Editorial Intelligence cycle.

---

## Core workflow types

**Signal capture workflows**
How to identify, record and file signals from research, customer conversations, events and market observation. The foundation of the Evidence Engine.

**Research activation workflows**
How to turn a research programme into a narrative — from initial findings through synthesis, positioning and editorial asset development.

**Customer story workflows**
How to conduct a customer conversation, extract the strategic evidence, write the story and connect it to the broader narrative it supports.

**Event intelligence workflows**
How to capture intelligence from events — before, during and after — and feed it back into the Evidence Engine as usable signal.

**Narrative development workflows**
How to build a new narrative from evidence: the process of identifying the pattern, developing the argument, testing it and expressing it across formats.

**AI-assisted editorial workflows**
How to use AI tools within the Editorial Intelligence system — where AI accelerates production, where editorial judgement still leads, and how to maintain quality and consistency across AI-assisted outputs.

---

## Why workflows compound

A workflow used once is a process. A workflow refined over many uses becomes organisational capability.

Each time a workflow is used, it generates feedback — what worked, what needed adjustment, what the next version should look like. That feedback is captured, and the workflow improves.

Over time, the Workflow Library becomes one of the most valuable assets in the Editorial Intelligence OS — not because of the individual workflows, but because of what they represent: accumulated learning about how to do the work well.

New team members start from the current best version of each workflow, not from scratch. AI-assisted processes are built on workflow foundations that have been tested and refined. The organisation's editorial capability improves systematically rather than through individual talent alone.

---

## The connection to AI-assisted editorial work

As AI becomes part of editorial production, workflows become more important, not less.

AI can execute a process at speed. Without a well-defined workflow, it executes the wrong process at speed. The quality of AI-assisted editorial work is determined by the quality of the workflow it operates within.

The Workflow Library creates the foundation for AI-assisted editorial production — the tested processes, quality standards and editorial logic that allow AI tools to accelerate output without degrading quality.

This is the basis of the [AI orchestration layer](/ai-systems): AI agents operating within editorial workflows defined and refined by human judgement.

## The five flagship frameworks

The Workflow Library sits within the Editorial Intelligence OS, which is built on five interconnected frameworks:

1. **Editorial Intelligence** — the discipline
2. **The Evidence Engine** — the infrastructure that feeds everything else
3. **Narrative Architecture** — how evidence becomes connected strategic position
4. **Content Activation** — how narratives reach buyers and AI systems
5. **AI Visibility & Buyability** — how reputation influences discovery and purchase decisions

Workflows are the operational layer that makes all five frameworks repeatable.
