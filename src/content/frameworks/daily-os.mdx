A framework describes how a system should work.

An operating rhythm is what makes it actually work.

The Editorial Intelligence cycle — signals, synthesis, narrative, activation, learning — only compounds if it runs consistently. The Daily OS is the operational layer that keeps it running: the regular practices, review cadences and decision points that turn a methodology into a working system.

Without this layer, even the best framework becomes theoretical. Teams understand the principles but drift back to campaign-by-campaign thinking. Signal capture happens occasionally rather than continuously. Narratives are developed once and left unchanged. The cycle stops compounding.

The Daily OS prevents that drift.

---

## The four rhythms

**Signal capture — continuous**

Signals are generated every day: customer conversations, market developments, product feedback, industry news, event observations, sales insights. Most are lost.

The Daily OS creates the habit and infrastructure to catch them. A regular discipline of noticing, recording and filing — brief enough to be sustainable, structured enough to be useful.

Not every signal is important. The discipline is in developing the editorial instinct to recognise which ones might be — and creating a low-friction way to record them before they disappear.

**Narrative review — weekly**

Narratives need tending. New evidence arrives that strengthens or refines a position. Market context shifts. Customer language evolves. A competitor claims adjacent territory.

A weekly narrative review is the practice of asking: what has changed this week, and does it affect any of our positions? It is brief — fifteen minutes, not two hours. Its purpose is to keep narratives current and ensure that new evidence is connected to the narrative it belongs to.

**Activation planning — monthly**

Once a month, the question shifts from maintaining the system to using it. What does the evidence base support activating next? Which narrative needs more expression? What format would serve it best? Who needs to be involved?

Monthly activation planning connects the evidence and narrative layers to the content calendar — but drives from narrative logic, not campaign requirements.

**Cycle review — quarterly**

Every quarter, a broader view. What has the Evidence Engine captured in the last three months? Which narratives have strengthened? Which have drifted or become dated? Where are the gaps?

The quarterly cycle review is where the Editorial Intelligence system reflects on itself — and where the next quarter's priorities are set from evidence rather than instinct.

---

## What the Daily OS is not

It is not a content calendar. Content calendars plan publication. The Daily OS manages the intelligence system that content is produced from.

It is not a project management tool. Project management tracks deliverables. The Daily OS tracks the health of the Editorial Intelligence cycle — whether signals are being captured, narratives are being maintained and evidence is accumulating.

It is not a reporting framework. Reporting describes what was produced. The Daily OS tracks whether the system is running — and whether the knowledge the organisation holds is becoming more valuable over time.

---

## The operational stack

The Daily OS operates across three levels.

**Individual** — the personal practices of anyone who feeds the Editorial Intelligence system. Signal capture habits. The discipline of filing observations before they disappear. The editorial instinct to connect what you're seeing to a narrative.

**Team** — the shared rhythms of the editorial or content team. Weekly narrative reviews. Monthly activation planning. The discipline of connecting individual signals to shared positions.

**Organisation** — the broader infrastructure that makes the cycle run at scale. The Evidence Engine. The Narrative Library. The Workflow Library. The connections between teams that would otherwise operate in silos.

The Daily OS makes these three levels work together — not through heavy process, but through lightweight, consistent rhythm.

---

## Starting simply

The full Daily OS does not need to be implemented at once.

Start with one practice: a weekly fifteen-minute narrative review. Ask what changed this week. Note one new signal. Check whether it connects to an existing position.

That single habit, maintained consistently, begins to create the conditions for compounding. As it becomes established, add signal capture infrastructure. Then monthly activation planning. Then the quarterly cycle review.

The Daily OS is not built in a day. It is built through consistent practice — which is exactly the point.
