*Active experiment. Webinar and Event Intelligence has been applied across Sage webinars, Accountex, FAB and Leaders in Accounting. The workflow is being tested and refined with each session and event cycle.*

---

## The problem

Webinars and events generate a concentrated burst of practitioner signal — what people are worried about, how they describe their work, what questions they keep asking, what frustrations keep surfacing.

Almost none of it is captured in a way that's reusable.

The standard pattern: run the session or attend the event, capture a few quotes for a social post, write a recap article, move on. The intelligence that emerged in those conversations disappears. Three months later, a brief lands asking for "what we learned at Accountex" or "what came out of the webinar series" and no one has a structured answer.

This isn't a content problem. It's an intelligence capture problem.

---

## The experiment

Webinar and Event Intelligence reframes the session or event as a signal-gathering system rather than a content activation moment.

The question it's testing: can practitioner conversations, speaker themes, audience questions and recurring frustrations — captured consistently across webinars and events — become inputs into narratives, frameworks, research and sales enablement that compound over time?

**Sessions and events used in this experiment**
- Sage webinar series (accounting and bookkeeping)
- Accountex (London and Manchester)
- FAB (Finance, Accounting and Bookkeeping Show)
- Leaders in Accounting

---

## How it works

```
Webinar or event
  ↓
Signals
(practitioner observations, recurring themes, audience questions, speaker claims)
  ↓
Patterns
(what's consistent across conversations and sessions)
  ↓
Narratives
(the strategic argument that the patterns support)
  ↓
Content, PR, sales enablement, customer stories, future research
  ↓
Learning loop
(what to test, probe and listen for in the next session or event)
```

The webinar or event becomes the start of a cycle, not a one-off moment.

---

## What this produced

The Accountex 2026 signal note documented the shift from episodic to continuous accounting work — a pattern that emerged from multiple conversations and sessions across two days. That observation fed directly into the Episodic to Continuous narrative, strengthened the Hidden Hours evidence base, and gave the MTD Continuous Model narrative a set of practitioner-voiced observations to work with.

Webinar sessions contributed a different kind of signal: structured audience questions and poll responses that revealed which aspects of a topic were generating the most uncertainty in practice. Both sources — live events and webinars — produced material that is still being used months later.

The signal note didn't produce a recap. It produced material that's still being used months later.

---

## What's being tested

- Whether a consistent capture template across webinars and events improves the quality and reusability of signals
- How much of the pattern identification can be accelerated with AI, and how much requires editorial judgement
- Whether webinar and event conversations can generate research hypotheses that feed into future survey design
- How sales teams can use webinar and event intelligence as a live briefing tool rather than a post-session report

---

## What this is not

Webinar and Event Intelligence is not a social listening workflow. It is not about monitoring brand mentions or tracking hashtags. It is not a content repurposing system. It does not cover webinar production, promotion or event management.

It is an attempt to make the practitioner intelligence gathered in webinars and in-person settings as durable and reusable as the research intelligence gathered in surveys.

The experiment is ongoing. The workflow is improving with each session and event cycle.
