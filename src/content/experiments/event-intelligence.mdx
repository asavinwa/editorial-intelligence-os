*Active experiment. Event Intelligence has been applied across Accountex, FAB and Leaders in Accounting. The workflow is being tested and refined with each event cycle.*

---

## The problem

Events generate a concentrated burst of practitioner signal — what people are worried about, how they describe their work, what questions they keep asking, what frustrations keep surfacing.

Almost none of it is captured in a way that's reusable.

The standard pattern: attend, capture a few quotes for a social post, write a recap article, move on. The intelligence that emerged in those conversations disappears. Three months later, a brief lands asking for "what we learned at Accountex" and no one has a structured answer.

Event content isn't a content problem. It's an intelligence capture problem.

---

## The experiment

Event Intelligence reframes the event as a signal-gathering system rather than a content activation moment.

The question it's testing: can practitioner conversations, speaker themes, audience questions and recurring frustrations — captured consistently across events — become inputs into narratives, frameworks, research and sales enablement that compound over time?

**Events used in this experiment**
- Accountex (London and Manchester)
- FAB (Finance, Accounting and Bookkeeping Show)
- Leaders in Accounting

---

## How it works

```
Event
  ↓
Signals
(practitioner observations, recurring themes, audience questions, speaker claims)
  ↓
Patterns
(what's consistent across conversations and sessions)
  ↓
Narratives
(the strategic argument that the patterns support)
  ↓
Content, PR, sales enablement, customer stories, future research
  ↓
Learning loop
(what to test, probe and listen for at the next event)
```

The event becomes the start of a cycle, not a one-off moment.

---

## What this produced

The Accountex 2026 signal note documented the shift from episodic to continuous accounting work — a pattern that emerged from multiple conversations and sessions across two days. That observation fed directly into the Episodic to Continuous narrative, strengthened the Hidden Hours evidence base, and gave the MTD Continuous Model narrative a set of practitioner-voiced observations to work with.

The signal note didn't produce a recap. It produced material that's still being used months later.

---

## What's being tested

- Whether a consistent capture template across events improves the quality and reusability of signals
- How much of the pattern identification can be accelerated with AI, and how much requires editorial judgement
- Whether events can generate research hypotheses that feed into future survey design
- How sales teams can use event intelligence as a live briefing tool rather than a post-event report

---

## What this is not

Event Intelligence is not a social listening workflow. It is not about monitoring brand mentions or tracking hashtags. It is not a content repurposing system.

It is an attempt to make the practitioner intelligence gathered in person as durable and reusable as the research intelligence gathered in surveys.

The experiment is ongoing. The workflow is improving with each event cycle.
